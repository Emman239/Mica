About Creative Connect Africa

A wellness-driven creative empowerment initiative dedicated to advancing mental health support, capacity building, and socio-economic inclusion for creatives across Africa.

Our Story

Creative Connect Africa emerged from a deep understanding of the mental health challenges facing creatives across the continent. Originally incubated under Mind Art Display Studio, we recognized that while Africa's creative sector is bursting with talent and innovation, many artists, musicians, writers, and creative entrepreneurs struggle with mental health issues that often go unaddressed.

As a registered Non-Governmental Organisation (NGO) in Nigeria, we believe that mental wellness is key to unlocking creative excellence and driving long-term cultural and economic impact across Africa.

Our Philosophy

We believe that inclusive access to wellness tools is critical—which is why our platform, resources, and programs are open to everyone, including students, aspiring artists, and all supporters who align with our mission.

Mental health advocacy shouldn't be a luxury or an afterthought in the creative industry. It should be foundational to how we nurture, develop, and sustain creative talent across Africa.

Our Vision & Mission

Vision

To build a mentally resilient and economically empowered creative community across Africa.

Mission

To create safe, inclusive, and sustainable spaces for African creatives to thrive through mental health advocacy, creative empowerment, and access to growth opportunities.

Who We Serve

Our target audience spans across the vibrant African creative ecosystem:

  • Young creatives (ages 18–35) across Africa
  • Freelancers and creative entrepreneurs
  • Students in creative fields
  • Creatives in underserved communities
  • General supporters of mental health and artistic innovation

Legal Status & Partnerships

Creative Connect Africa operates as a registered Non-Governmental Organisation (NGO) in Nigeria, originally incubated under Mind Art Display Studio. We work closely with strategic partners including Blue Vision Media, creative industry hubs and schools, mental health professionals, and advocacy and community-based organizations across Africa.

Our collaborative approach ensures that we can deliver comprehensive support that addresses both the creative and mental health needs of our community members.
